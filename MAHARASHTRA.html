The Lezim is a traditional folk percussion instrument from Maharashtra, often associated with the state's popular Lezim dance. It is typically used during celebrations, especially in New Year and festival celebrations, like Gudi Padwa (Maharashtrian New Year).

üïâÔ∏è Cultural Use: **Lezim dance**, **festivals**, **traditional folk performances**

üé≠ Performance Style: Played while dancing or in a group, often accompanied by other percussion instruments

The **Lezim** is not just a musical instrument but also a dance prop in the **Lezim dance**, a vibrant and energetic performance that is often seen during the **Gudi Padwa festival**. It symbolizes joy, prosperity, and celebration, and is an essential part of Maharashtra's cultural festivals.
