Idakka
+

The Idakka is a fascinating percussion instrument from Kerala, known for its unique ability to produce both rhythmic beats and melodic tones. It is an hourglass-shaped drum, similar to the damaru, but played with a stick instead of rattling cords.

Key Features of the Idakka:

Construction: Made from wood (often jackfruit or sandalwood) with tightly stretched drumheads, allowing pitch modulation.

Playing Technique: Suspended over the left shoulder and played with a curved stick in the right hand.

Sound & Acoustics: Unlike most drums, the Idakka can produce definite pitches, making it both a rhythmic and melodic instrument.

Cultural Significance: Used in temple rituals, especially during Sopanam music, and accompanies classical dance forms like Kathakali and Mohiniyattam Symbolism: Some traditions associate its structure with cosmic elements, representing the sun, moon, and spiritual forces.
