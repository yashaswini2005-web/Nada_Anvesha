UTTER KHAND's

Traditional Musical Instruments

🥁
Damru
+

The Damru is an hourglass-shaped drum associated with Lord Shiva and spiritual rituals. Played by shaking, creating rhythmic beats with striking beads.

  • 🎵 Instrument Type: Percussion (Hand Drum)
  • 🌳 Material: Wood or clay with animal hide or synthetic heads
  • 🎼 Played With: Shaken to strike beads on drumheads
  • 📏 Shape: Hourglass
  • 🔊 Sound: Sharp and resonant
  • 🕉️ Cultural Use: Hindu rituals, folk music, temple ceremonies
  • 🎭 Performance Style: Solo or with chants/devotional songs

🌟 Cultural Importance: Symbolizes cosmic creation. Played during Mahashivratri and rituals to invoke divine energy.

🎺
Turri
+

The Turri is a traditional wind instrument from Gujarat, made of bamboo or wood, producing melodic and expressive tones used in folk and religious settings.

  • 🎵 Instrument Type: Wind (Flute-like)
  • 🌳 Material: Bamboo or wood
  • 🎼 Played With: Blown through mouthpiece
  • 📏 Shape: Cylindrical tube with finger holes
  • 🔊 Sound: Soft, flowing, nostalgic
  • 🕉️ Cultural Use: Festivals, weddings, temple rituals
  • 🎭 Performance Style: Solo or in folk ensembles

🌟 Cultural Importance: Deeply connected to Gujarat’s folk tales and traditions, symbolizes cultural identity and unity.
