NAGALAND's

Traditional Musical Instruments

🎸
Tati
+

The Tati is a traditional single-stringed instrument used by the Angami, Chakhesang, and Mao Naga tribes.

Key Features:

  • 🎵 Plucked string instrument
  • 🌳 Made from dried gourd and animal bladder film
  • 📏 Long pole with a resonating gourd
  • 🔊 Soft, expressive sound

Cultural Significance:
Used for storytelling, festivals, and traditional Lu / Li / Lo songs, the Tati reflects deep Naga heritage.

📯
Bamboo Trumpet
+

The Bamboo Trumpet is a loud wind instrument used by herdsmen and during tribal ceremonies.

Key Features:

  • 🎵 Wind instrument
  • 🌳 Made from bamboo
  • 📏 Long cylindrical shape
  • 🔊 Powerful, deep sound

Cultural Significance:
Used for cattle herding and in rituals, it represents practical and symbolic values in Naga tradition.

🎶
Bamboo Mouth Organ
+

The Bamboo Mouth Organ is a delicate wind instrument played mainly in dormitories called Zuki.

Key Features:

  • 🎵 Wind instrument with reeds
  • 🌳 Made of bamboo pipes
  • 📏 Small, handheld shape
  • 🔊 Soft, melodic sound

Cultural Significance:
Known as the "Midnight Musical Instrument", it promotes relaxation and is a key symbol of youth gatherings and harmony.
